Merriman Capital Initiates on Pegasystems Inc. with a Buy Rating

Merriman Holdings Inc. May 15, 2014 1:29 PM
0 shares
Content preferences

     

    SAN FRANCISCO - May 15, 2014 - Merriman Capital, Inc. ("Merriman"), a wholly owned subsidiary of Merriman Holdings, Inc. (MERR), announced that it has initiated equity research coverage of Pegasystems Inc. (PEGA) with a Buy rating and a $24 price target.

    Pegasystems, Inc. (www.pega.com) provides software and professional services to automate and manage complex, continually evolving business processes and customer interactions. The company`s software helps businesses engage customers, simplify operations, and quickly adapt their enterprise applications to a rapidly changing market environment.

    Pegasystems, Inc. was founded in 1983 and completed an initial public offering in July, 1996, raising $29 million. In January 1997, the company raised an additional $52 million with a follow-on offering. Inspired partially by the founder`s earlier experience in teaching computers how to play chess, PEGA pioneered the development of business process management software and rules engine platforms. Alan Trefler is the founder, CEO and chairman of the board, as well as the company`s largest shareholder with a 52% ownership interest. Rafe Brown joined the company, as CFO, in September, 2013, after spending nine years at Salesforce.com (NASDAQ:CRM, $51.88, NR).

    Following several years of sluggish revenue growth (4% revenue CAGR from 2000-2005), PEGA changed its go-to-market strategy in 2005, subsequent to a complete re-architecting of the core product. From a go-to-market perspective, the company separated the sales of applications from its core BPM platform, allowing for smaller initial deal sizes and a more simplified sales process, which also helped to increase the participation of third-party service providers. Additionally, the company moved to a "named account" sales model and migrated from an all-or-nothing RFP-style sales process to "foot-in-the-door" deals much more limited in scope, with modest initial license sales and more regular follow-on business. Following the change in strategy, financial performance improved and PEGA steadily evolved from a niche provider into the world`s largest pure-play BPM vendor. Over the past eight years, PEGA has posted a 23% (largely organic) revenue CAGR, growing from $100 million in sales in 2005 to north of $500 million in 2013.
