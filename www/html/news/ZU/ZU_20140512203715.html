    IPO Surge Makes Zulily Founder a Billionaire

    comments, called-out
    Comment Now
    Follow Comments Following Comments Unfollow Comments
    Comment Now
    Follow Comments Following Comments Unfollow Comments

    Mark Vadon, founder of Zulily, is the latest retail billionaire.

    Zulily, an online vendor of merchandise geared toward moms and children, may not yet be a household name like Facebook and Twitter, but it sure has resonated with investors. The company went public last Friday at $22 per share, and bulls drove the price up 71% to $37.70 per share by the end of first day trading. In the process, it made a billionaire out of founder Mark Vadon.

    Vadon, 43, owns 34.1 million shares of the Seattle-based company, or a little more than 30%. With Zulily trading at about $35.50 per share, that makes his stake worth north of $1.2 billion.

    But Vadon, a Harvard alum with an MBA from Stanford, is likely worth a little bit more than that. He founded online jewelry retailer Blue Nile in 1999 and took that company public in 2004 at $20.5 per share. He owned about 10% of the company at the time, or 1.7 million shares, but has since sold most of that stake. He still serves on the board as chairman, but is stepping down at the end of the year. Before that he worked as a consultant for Bain & Company from 1992 to 1999.

    Vadon isn’t the only person to get filthy rich from Zulily’s big IPO. CEO and cofounder Darrell Cavens, 43, owns more than 21 million shares of the company, worth about $750 million. He also worked with Vadon at Blue Nile.

    Zulily could not comment because of the post-IPO quiet period.
