Major Greek players invest in National Bank of Greece


Greece's largest lender National Bank (NBG) priced its 2.5 billion euro share offering at 2.20 euros a share after books closed on Thursday, an official at the bank told Reuters.

"The price that will be proposed to the shareholders meeting on May 10 will be 2.20 euros a share," the official said, declining to be named.

Reports in the Greek press suggest major Greek shipowners are amongst investors putting faith - and vast amounts of money for that matter - in NBG. The names of S. Latsis, E. Marinakis, J. Coustas, N. Pateras, G. Economou and A. Mistakidis have surfaced amongst foreign funds such as Pimco, BlackRock, Fidelity and JP Morgan that have also reportedly invested in the Greek bank.

NBG is the latest Greek lender to tap international investors to cover a 2.18 billion euro capital hole revealed in a central bank stress test in March.
