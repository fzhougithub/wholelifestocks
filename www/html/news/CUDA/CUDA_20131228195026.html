Barracuda Networks Tops 6 New IPOs Debuting Wed.

Wed, Nov 06 2013 00:00:00 E 00_WEB

By BRIAN DEAGON, INVESTOR'S BUSINESS DAILY

 Posted 11/06/2013 02:33 PM ET

  • Email
  • Print
  • License
  • Comment

Six of an expected 15 IPOs this week launched Wednesday, with Barracuda Networks (CUDA) being the top performer.

Barracuda Networks helps companies address security threats, improve network performance and data management using a cloud-based architecture. It is one of two tech IPOs to debut Wednesday, along with Wix.com (WIX).

Cloud companies are dominating tech initial public offerings in numbers and performance, as IBD has reported.

Barracuda Networks priced at $18, the low end of its expected range, as the company raised $75 million. Barracuda stock opened at 22 and was up 16% in afternoon trading in the stock market Wednesday.

For the six months ended Aug. 31, Barracuda reported revenue of $114 million, up 20% from the year-earlier period, and a net loss of $4.9 million.

Silicon Valley-based Barracuda is a big sponsor of the San Jose Sharks National Hockey League team.

Wix.com priced at $16.50, the high end of its range, and opened at 18.50. It raised $127 million. But Wix stock was down a fraction in afternoon trading. The Tel Aviv-based company provides a cloud-based website development platform, and has 39 million registered users worldwide. Its competitors include Wordpress.org.

For the nine months ended Sept. 30, it reported revenue of $55.5 million, up 82%, and a net loss of $17.8 million.

The largest IPO Wednesday was Latin America airlines company Avianca Holdings (AVH), which raised $409 million selling 27.2 million shares at $15, the low end of its range. It was down 6%.

There was one biotech, Karyopharm Therapeutics (KPTI), an early-stage developer of treatments for cancer. It raised $109 million. Karyopharm priced at $16 and was up a fraction.

Another was Blue Capital Reinsurance Holdings (BCRH), a Bermuda reinsurance holding company that offers collateralized reinsurance in the property catastrophe market. Blue Capital priced at $20 and raised $125 million. Shares were down 7%.

Arc Logistics Partners (ARCX), which owns crude oil and refined product terminals, raised $114 million by offering 6 million shares at $19, the low end of the range, and was down a fraction.

Twitter (TWTR) is set to begin trading on the NYSE Thursday. If Twitter prices at $25, the high end of its expected range, and sells all 70 million shares plus the 10.5 million extra shares allotted to underwriters, the company would raise about $2.01 billion with its initial public offering, as IBD reported.
