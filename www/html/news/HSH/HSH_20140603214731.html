Hillshire to talk with Pilgrim’s Pride, Tyson Foods

Board not making any recommendation on competiting bids

By Michael Calia

Hillshire Brands Co. on Tuesday said it would hold separate talks with Pilgrim’s Pride Corp. and Tyson Foods Inc., who have each offered to buy the maker of Jimmy Dean sausage and Sara Lee desserts.

Hillshire’s /quotes/zigman/10406668/delayed/quotes/nls/hsh HSH +9.48%   announcement comes after The Wall Street Journal reported Monday night that Pilgrim’s Pride /quotes/zigman/13509380/delayed/quotes/nls/ppc PPC -2.24%   had raised its bid for Hillshire to $55 a share, topping Tyson’s /quotes/zigman/244186/delayed/quotes/nls/tsn TSN -3.04%   $50-a-share offer. Pilgrim’s Pride had initially offered $45 a share for Hillshire last month before Tyson got in on the action.

Hillshire said its board isn’t making any recommendation regarding the Pilgrim’s Pride and Tyson bids, and that it would provide information to both companies.

Hillshire, however, said its board isn’t “withdrawing, modifying, withholding or qualifying its recommendation” with regard to the company’s own bid to buy Pinnacle Foods Inc. /quotes/zigman/14664120/delayed/quotes/nls/pf PF +3.21%  

Hillshire unveiled plans last month to buy Pinnacle for $4.3 billion, while also agreeing to pay a $163 million breakup fee if it didn’t follow through with the deal.
