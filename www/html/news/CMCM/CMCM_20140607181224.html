IPO Stock Watch: Four Recent IPOs Hit New Highs

Fri, Jun 06 2014 00:00:00 E 00_WEB

By BRIAN DEAGON, INVESTOR'S BUSINESS DAILY

 Posted 06/06/2014 06:09 PM ET

  • Email
  • Print
  • Reprints
  • Comment

Four recent tech IPOs hit new highs Friday, with Cheetah Mobile (NYSE:CMCM) showing the biggest pop, with TrueCar (NASDAQ:TRUE) racing close behind.

JPMorgan initiated coverage on Cheetah Mobile Friday with an overweight, or buy, rating and a price target of $26. Cheetah Mobile stock rose 22%, or 3.56, to close at 19.75, in the stock market today.

Cheetah Mobile, a spinoff of China-based software company Kingsoft, provides a suite of free mobile and PC applications that offer security and anti-virus protection. Cheetah launched its initial public offering on May 8, priced at 14, and raised $168 million.

The 25-day, SEC-enforced quiet period on Cheetah expired on June 1, allowing its IPO underwriters to release research reports on the firm. The underwriters of the Cheetah Mobile IPO were JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley and Credit Suisse.

The SEC enforced quiet period on TrueCar expires June 10. TrueCar stock on Friday rose 21%, or 2.53, and closed at 14.54. TrueCar operates an online platform allowing consumers to get guaranteed pricing on new cars.

TrueCar launched its IPO May 16 priced at 9, well below its expected range of 12 to 14. It raised $70 million by offering 7.8 million shares. The lead underwriters of TrueCar's IPO were Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan and RBC Capital Markets.

Also closing at a new high Friday was China-based online beauty products retailer Jumei (NYSE:JMEI). Its quiet period ends Monday. Jumei stock rose 5% to close at 29.69.

Jumei launched its IPO on May 16, priced at 22 and above the high end of its range. Its underwriters were Goldman Sachs, Credit Suisse and JPMorgan.

China-based JD.com (NASDAQ:JD) also closed at a new high Friday. It rose 3% to 26.58. The e-commerce giant launched its IPO on May 22, priced at 19.

Meanwhile, Arista Networks (NYSE:ANET) rose 28% to 55 in Friday's trading debut after pricing its IPO at 43, above the expected price range of 36-40.
