Zalicus: Where From Here?

Nov. 15, 2013 12:02 PM ET |  by: Stock Whisper About: ZLCS

Zalicus Inc. is a company focused on developing treatments for pain. It has an FDA-approved non-opioid painkiller Exalgo, being marketed by Mallinckrodt, from whom Zalicus receives royalties on Exalgo sales. The company has a sole pain product candidate in its pipeline. The company is going through a rather rough patch, following the drop of its blockbuster potential candidate Z160, due to failure in trials.

The Business

Zalicus Inc. (ZLCS), a biopharmaceutical company, focuses on developing and discovering treatments for patients suffering from pain. The proprietary pipeline of the company consists of Z944, a T-type calcium modulator for pain, and modulator of Nav 1.7 channel also for pain. It uses its proprietary cHTS technology and Chalice software to assist the discovery of new candidates. The company has entered into collaborations in the area of pain, oncology, inflammation and infectious diseases, and receives royalties and grants.

Zalicus' competitors include Actavis plc (ACT), Cephalon Inc., and Novartis AG.

Technology

The company has a proprietary combination high throughput screening (cHTS) platform that is highly integrated and automated and allows screening myriad drug combinations across large panels of well annotated cell lines. The platform is employed in discovering additional candidates for the company and also to supplement collaborative partners' pipelines.

Zalicus also has an analytic software, Chalice Analyzer, used to characterize single-agent and multi-target screening data. It further allows for the quantitative assesment of interactions' strength. The software may be used for the entire organization, for individual scientists or online web results, customized accordingly.

Collaborations

Zalicus considers collaborations as an important part of its business strategy, for the development and commercialization of selective product candidates. It has collaborated with Mallinckrodt for the commercialization of Exalgo®; and with Sanofi's division Fovea Pharmaceuticals for the development of Prednisporin by them.

Novartis and Zalicus entered into a strategic alliance for discovery of anti-cancer combinations, in May 2009. Both the companies will contribute their compounds from their compounds library and the anti-cancer effects will be evaluated using Zalicus' proprietary cHTSTM platform and analyzer software Chalice. Zalicus received $4 million in upfront payment and is eligible to receive almost $58 million in milestone payments.

Zalicus and Hydra Biosciences Inc., in February 2012, entered in a research collaboration agreement for advancing the development of Zalicus' preclinical ion channel modulator candidates to treat pain. The research and development activities will be funded by Zalicus for two year period.

The U.S. Army Medical Research Institute for Infectious Diseases (USAMRIID) has conferred $3.5 million in funded research grants to Zalicus for the discovery and treatment of various biothreat agents, including viral hemorrhagic fevers (VHF). VHF is caused by a distinct family of viruses that impact multiple organ systems of the body and may be accompanied by bleeding (hemorrhage).

Candidates

The proprietary candidate of Zalicus awaiting initiation of phase II of clinical development is Z944, an oral non-opioid pain drug. It is an oral T-type calcium channel modulator being developed to treat chronic and acute pain. The T-type calcium channels are also found in heart, endocrine cells, brain and other tissues, providing a possibility for developing therapeutics for varied indications that include treating pain. The drug is protected under U.S. patent, covering the methods used for treating pain, until 2029.

Another class of ion channel being pursued by Zalicus is the Voltage-gated sodium channels ((Nav)) that regulate pain signaling. The sodium ion currents, which contribute to the excitability of peripheral pain-sensing neurons and the neurons in the spinal cord relaying pain signals to the brain, are mediated by Nav. It is still in the preclinical stage and is being studied with Hydro Biosciences Inc.

Exalgo, an FDA-approved drug, is used for the treatment of moderate to severe pain in opioid tolerant patients. The drug requires one daily dosage, as compared to other treatments requiring several doses a day. The commercialization rights for U.S. rest with Mallinckrodt, while Zalicus receives royalty payments for the sales. It received $1.5 million in the third quarter of 2013, ending September 30.

Zalicus has licensed its topical ocular drug candidate - Prednisporin for the treatment of inflammatory ophthalmic diseases, to Sanofi's division Fovea Pharmaceuticals SA, for development. Prednisporin is in phase II/b of clinical trial for treating persistent allergic conjunctivitis.

Market Performance

The mean price target for the company is $6.40 for the year and a low of $1.20 and a high of $12, with a current price of $1.21 as of November 13, 2012. The company's share price took a 75% plunge on Monday (11/11), following the drop of Z160 after trial failure, and are trading below its low price target. The mean analyst recommendation is to hold the stock.

The year to date performance of Zalicus is -69.7% against a 25.04% performance of S&P. The share prices of the company remained volatile and traded below $1 for most part of the year. The performance improved after the 1:6 stock split and the positive results of Z160, but plunged again when the trial for Z160 was discontinued.

Upcoming Events

The company doesn't have major upcoming catalysts. It is expected to initiate phase II clinical trials of Z944 in the first quarter of 2014 and the data is estimated to be available in the second half. The success of the trials may prove to be the saving grace of the company.

Fundamentals and Potential Risks

The company recorded revenues of $3.4 million in the third quarter, a $0.1 million decrease from the comparable quarter of 2012. The revenue missed analyst estimates by $0.26 million. The revenue included a $1.5 million of royalties from sales of Exalgo. The net loss for the third quarter was $10.5 million, a 13.5% decrease when compared to the relative period of 2012. The company incurred $9.3 million in R&D expenses, with approximately $5.5 million for phase II studies of Z160, which were ultimately discontinued. The company has cash and equivalents of $20 million, with an estimated burn of approximately $6 to $7 million.

From November 15, 2013, the generic versions, 8 mg, 12 mg and 16 mg doses of Exalgo will be allowed for sale by third party manufacturers. And by mid-May next year the 32 mg doses will also be allowed. These events will have a negative impact on the royalty received by Zalicus from sales. Overall, Mallinckrodt expects the sales of Exalgo to drop in the year 2014.

The share prices of the company have dipped substantially, and if the trend continues and it goes below $1, the company will have to do a reverse split to keep it listed on NASDAQ. Furthermore, the company may require additional funds, and they might have to do so through dilution. Hence, one of the two options is inevitable in my opinion.

Bottom-line

The discontinuation of Z160 development had a major impact on the stock prices of the company and the millions spent on its development have gone to waste. The Z160, as reported by the company, failed to meet the clinical efficacy. This is the second trial of Zalicus that has failed, the previous occurring just last year i.e. Synavive, for the treatment of immuno-inflammatory diseases. Investors' trust in the future trials has dropped substantially and the company will have to provide positive news sometimes soon to restore its position.

Despite the failure of second trial of Zalicus in two years, the sentiment surrounding the company is still hopeful that the company will rise from the ashes. The pain candidate Z944 is still touted to be a major success if it provides positive results in its future trials.

The financial position of the money isn't very rosy, but does have royalty payments from the sales and the collaborations. The company does have a marketed product and collaborations at the right places, with proprietary technology, and it just might have a chance.

The stock price of the company has bottomed and are expected to bounce back from this state, but this is a risk. The stock is a risky investment and doesn't have any positive news imminent and may not see a major rally. Those who have the money to wait a long time for the company to restore itself may want to hold the stock. Overall, only those with a high risk appetite may want to invest in the stock at the moment.
