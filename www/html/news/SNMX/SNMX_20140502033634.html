Senomyx's CEO Discusses Q1 2014 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

May. 1, 2014 3:02 PM ET  |  About: SNMX by: SA Transcripts

Gwen Rosenberg - VP, IR and Corporate Communications

John Poyhonen - CEO

Senomyx, Inc. (SNXM) Q1 2014 Earnings Conference Call May 1, 2013 11:00 AM ET

Operator

Good morning. We will now begin the Senomyx Conference Call. At this time, I would like to inform you that this conference is being recorded and that all participants are in a listen-only mode. At the request of the company, we will open the conference up for questions-and-answers after the presentation. (Operator Instructions). I would now like to turn the call over to Gwen Rosenberg, Senomyx's Vice President of Investor Relations and Corporate Communications.

Gwen Rosenberg

Good morning and welcome to the Senomyx first quarter 2014 earnings and corporate update conference call. Participating on this call from Senomyx will be John Poyhonen, Chief Executive Officer; Sharon Wicker, Senior Vice President, Chief Commercial Development Officer; Tony Rogers, Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer and Don Karanewsky, Senior Vice President Discovery and Chief Scientific Officer.

Before we begin, please note that during the course of this call, we may make projections or other forward-looking statements regarding future events or financial performance of the company that involve risks and uncertainties. The company's actual results may differ materially from the projections described in this morning's press release and this conference call.

Factors that might cause such a difference include, but are not limited to those discussed in our quarterly and annual reports filed with the SEC. Copies of these documents are available upon request from Investor Relations at Senomyx or may be accessed via our web site, at www.senomyx.com.

I'd now like to turn the discussion over to John Poyhonen, CEO of Senomyx.

John Poyhonen

Thank you Gwen, good morning to everyone and thank you joining the Senomyx Management team for our conference call and webcast. During our last earnings call we introduced three key areas that our efforts will be focused on during 2014. The first quarter of the year was very eventful for Senomyx and we'll start this call with progress that we've made in each of these three focus areas.

First we're continuing to grow, protect and maximize our unmatched expertise and taste science technologies. Senomyx's proprietary technology platform has enabled discovery as an innovative flavor ingredients in the areas of sweet, savory, bitter and cooling taste. We're building on this success with our current research activities. And our Sweet Taste program Senomyx is working to discover additional novel flavors with modifying properties that could create incremental value such as allowing for an even greater reduction of sweeteners or lower cost and use at our current sweet flavor modifiers.

We're evaluating a number of new sucrose and fructose modifiers that posses advantageous properties in our potential candidates for development activities. We have also identified several natural sucrose modifiers and natural high intensity sweeteners that provide taste proof of concept and validate the methodologies we're using for the natural products components of our sweet taste program.

Ongoing activities in this project includes scale up of additional material of the most promising natural discoveries to further characterize the taste and physical properties. This evaluation process will be important as we assess the viability of our current discoveries. In addition we're continuing our efforts related to further expansion of Senomyx's natural product library, (tight group) [ph] of screening of selected (plant to rock) [ph] samples and [indiscernible] with samples of interest.

In our cooling program we're moving forward with development activities for S2227 a novel cooling agent that has aroma and taste advantages over currently available agents. We're also evaluating additional new cooling agents discovered by Senomyx for potential future development.

Senomyx's salt taste program is an important part of the Company's research activities. Our current objective is to discover the specific protein or group of proteins that function to detect salt in foods. This protein or proteins can then serve as research targets to discover new salt flavor modifiers using Senomyx's flavor technologies.

Starting with the large proprietary database of proteins found in taste buds Senomyx has identified a small group of proteins that meet certain criteria or potentially being involved in salt taste. We're making progress as we evaluate each of these candidate targets to see if modifiers can be identified and lead us to a taste proof concept which would validate a salty taste target. The ultimate goal is to indentify flavors with modifying properties that can restore the desired salty taste in products with reduced salt.

In April Senomyx announced that we had entered into a new research agreement with PepsiCo related to the salt taste program. PepsiCo has been a value partner for our Sweet Taste program and we're very pleased to extend our relationship into an additional taste area.

This is a new type of research agreement for Senomyx with both near-term funding and potential long term benefits for the company. PepsiCo will contribute funding for the salt taste program throughout 2014 and they have options to continue the research funding for up to three additional years.

PepsiCo will have non-exclusive rights to salt flavor modifiers discovered during the research funding period for use in all of their products worldwide.

In addition, they will receive a two year period of exclusivity for use, of each of these flavor modifiers in snack foods and snack dips. Importantly salt flavor modifiers discovered under this agreement could be added to Senomyx's a direct portfolio.

We retain significant freedom to operate which will allow Senomyx to market these salt flavor modifiers as a solution for a company seeking to retain great taste in a wide variety of lower sodium products.

In addition we have the right to supply the salt flavor modifiers directly to PepsiCo. This provision will allow Senomyx to establish commercial manufacturing capabilities through a third party which will be beneficial to our direct sales activities.

Senomyx's second area of focus is the expansion of our flavor ingredients pipeline for collaborators and flavor industry customers. Senomyx flavors are unique in their ability to create a competitive advantage by enabling an improved nutritional profile, better taste or lower cost in a broad range of food and beverage products.

As we discussed in our March call, our S617 Sweetmyx flavor ingredient is the most recent and potentially the most valuable of all our ingredients discovered to date. It has utility for our broad range of non-alcoholic beverages, alcoholic beverages in a wide variety of foods. The recent FEMA GRAS regulatory designation allows immediate use of the Sweetmyx flavor ingredient in the U.S. and a number of other countries.

While we cannot discuss any specifics regarding commercialization plans at this time, Senomyx is working with our partners as they evaluate potential product opportunities for the new Sweetmyx flavor ingredient.

PepsiCo has exclusive rights to use this ingredient worldwide in non-alcoholic beverages, Firmenich has commercialization rights for food products and alcoholic beverages with exclusive rights until March 2018. At which time Senomyx will be able to market Sweetmyx S617 to flavor companies for food applications.

We've also extended our current flavoring ingredient portfolio for Senomyx's direct sales customers. During the past quarter we began introducing additional flavor offerings to flavor companies thereby broadening our ability to address their needs for innovative flavor solutions.

Senomyx's third area of focus is the successful implementation of the direct sales program, as measured by our ability to drive commercial revenues. Our direct sales efforts involved, presenting Senomyx's ingredients to flavor companies that create proprietary flavor systems for their food and beverage company clients.

We are very encouraged by the response from flavor companies that have begun in valuations of our ingredients.

I will now turn the call over to Sharon Wicker who will address Senomyx's commercialization activities. She will be followed by Tony Rogers who will review our financial status and outlook and I will return after the Q&A section for a few closing remarks at the end of the call. Sharon?

Sharon Wicker

Thank you, John. We are excited about Senomyx's direct sales program which is gaining momentum with further evaluations of our ingredients high flavor companies, including addition of the new offerings that John just mentioned.

Our Complimyx taste modulation ingredients brand now includes two Senomyx's Sweetmyx sucrose modifiers, as well as a Savorymyx savory flavor and a Bittermyx Bitter Blocker. Commercialization activities were initiated recently with Sweetmyx SR69, also known as S6973, which is used to maintain the sweet taste in reduced-sugar products.

Two key benefits of Sweetmyx SR69 are that it has been granted regulatory approvals in nearly every major country, and it is the first sucrose modifier in our direct sales portfolio being promoted for use in food products.

SR69 is applicable for use in numerous food product categories, including baked goods, confectionaries, cereals and dairy products, such as yogurt and ice cream. According to [indiscernible] monitor global use of sucrose across targeted food product categories is approximately 17 million metric tons on an annual basis.

We estimate that our existing regulatory approvals allow for the commercialization at SR69 in more than 90% of the target market opportunity. We expect to receive initial commercial scale quantities of SR69 from our manufacturer by mid-year 2014. SR69 is an excellent complement to Sweetmyx, SR96 also known as S9632 another effective sucrose modifier that we are currently marketing for use with reduced sugar beverages.

Targeted product categories for SR96, include ready-to-drink and powdered forms of coffee, tea and flavored milk, powdered soft drinks and dairy beverages. According to our research, these target product categories use more than 5.2 million metric tons of sucrose on an annual basis.

We estimate that each global market share point is worth approximately $8 million to us in direct sales revenues. Furthermore, we estimate that our current regulatory approvals allow for the commercialization of SR96 in approximately 30% of the target market opportunity at this time.

Commercial scale quantities of SR96 have been received from our contract manufacturer. SR69 and SR96 are each highly affected in restoring the desired flavor profile of products where sugar has been reduced. Having both of these novel ingredients in our Sweetmyx portfolio allows Senomyx to address multiple food and beverage product requirements and become a valuable resource for our flavor company customers. We are also pleased with the feedback from flavor companies to Savorymyx UM80. This novel savory flavor can be used to replace monosodium glutamate or MSG and can also be combined with other flavor ingredients to create unique savory flavor blends.

UM80 has virtually global regulatory approvals and is applicable for a wide variety of products including sauces, seasoning blends, frozen foods, cooking aids, soups and snack food. UM80 is very versatile and can also allow manufacturers to achieve a cost savings compared to MSG. MSG consumption during 2012 was more than 2.4 million metric tonnes. We estimate that each global market share point is worth approximately $2 million to us in direct sales revenues. In addition we estimate that our existing regulatory approvals allow for the commercialization of UM80 in more than 90% of the target market opportunity.

Evaluations of UM80 are underway in the U.S. and other geographies for use in a range of food and beverage product categories. Senomyx has received commercial inventory quantities of UM80 from a global manufacturer to support our sales and marketing efforts. Most recently Senomyx has started selling our Bittermyx BB68 Bitter Blockers to potential flavor company customers.

BB68 can be used as part of a flavor system for foods and beverages to reduce the bitterness of ingredients such as hydrolyzed soy and whey proteins, menthol, caffeine, cocoa, and stevia reducing the bitterness of high-protein products is especially helpful for manufacturers of value added nutritionally based products.

In summary we are making good headway with our efforts to leverage Senomyx's proprietary taste science technologies to become a leading commercial provider of novel flavor ingredients to the flavor industry. Feedback from the flavor companies has been positive and we believe we've had an excellent start towards this goal. More specifically we continue to receive a high level of interest from our customers to conduct evaluations across all of our flavor offerings.

Additionally many of our customers have requested price quotations and access to complete supplier authorization surveys. Both of these actions are typically required before purchase orders can be placed. We believe that this activity along with the modest initial orders we have received from flavor companies represent encouraging signs with the future placement of larger orders.

I look forward to providing further updates regarding our direct sales on our next earnings call.

Turning to our royalty based collaborations; Senomyx continues to receive commercial revenues from Firmenich, Nestlé and Ajinomoto on sales of their products incorporating our flavor ingredients. In brief as indicated in today's press release, our partners are continuing to conduct commercialization activities with Senomyx's S6973 and S9632 sucrose modifiers as well as S2383 a modifier of the commonly used high intensity sweeteners sucralose S336, the savory flavor and our S6821 Bitter Blocker.

I will now turn the discussion to Tony Rogers Senomyx's CFO who will provide an overview of our financial status and outlook. Tony.

Tony Rogers

Thank you Sharon. Financial results for the first quarter of 2014 met or exceeded management expectations and we're on track to achieve our financial guidance. At March 31, 2014 Senomyx held $31.6 million in cash, cash equivalents and investments available for sale. Total revenues were $8.2 million for the first quarter ended March 31, 2014 compared to 7.5 million for the same period in 2013. The increase primarily resulted from $1.3 million of development milestones earned in the first quarter of 2014 related to the Sweet Taste program.

This increase was partially offset by a decrease in development revenues related to Senomyx's cooling taste program as research fundings under the company's cooling taste program collaboration with Firmenich expired in June 2013. Total commercial revenues in the first quarter 2014 were $1.4 million which were primarily from our royalty based collaborations. During the first quarter we recorded revenue associated with direct sales to flavor companies.

As expected, these initial sales were modest, we continue to make promising incremental progress and we expect the direct sales will be a key driver of commercial revenue growth in the future. Research, development and patents expense including stock based expenses were $7 million for the first quarter of 2014 compared to $7.4 million in the first quarter of 2013. The primary factor for the decrease from 2013 to 2014 was reduced outside services cost for safety study to support regulatory filings.

Selling, general and administrative expenses were 3.1 million for the first quarter of 2014 and 3 million for the first quarter of 2013. The increase was primarily due to increased non-cash stock based expenses in 2014 resulting from a higher fair value for stock options granted in 2014 based on the higher price of Senomyx's common stock. The net loss for the quarter ended March 31, 2014 improved to $0.05 per share compared to $0.07 per share for the quarter ended March 31, 2013.

Regarding our financial guidance for the full year 2014 Senomyx continues to expect total revenues of $32 million to $35 million of which approximately 10 million are commercial revenues. Our commercial revenues will be weighted towards the end of the year and our guidance assumes S617 will be commercialized by one of our partners in the second half of 2014.

We also expect total operating expenses of 44 million to 46 million of which approximately 6 million is non-cash stock based expense. Our net loss is anticipated to be 10 million to 12 million resulting in basic and diluted net loss of $0.23 to $0.28 per share. We expect to end the year with more than $25 million in cash, cash equivalents and investments available for sale. With $31.6 million in cash, no debt and significant development funding commitment from our collaborators going forward, Senomyx remains well positioned to achieve our discovery development commercialization objectives including our goal to achieve approximately $25 million in commercial revenues in 2015 and importantly our goal to achieve profitability in 2015.

I will now turn the call back over to the operator to open up for questions.

Good morning Scott, this is John. I think that really the key developments that we've had in the first four months of the year really are in three areas starting with the approval of Sweetmyx S617 as you know we received (thermograph) [ph] determination of that and announced that in March. That's critical for us as a company it's our first sweet modifier that works both with sucrose and fructose and allows for a wide use in a wide range of non-alcoholic beverages as well as alcoholic beverages and food product. So I think that stands out as the number one accomplishments.

Number two, I think we're very excited about the new products that we've added to our direct sales portfolio with the approval of S617 we were able to regain rights from Firmenich of SR69, our sucrose modifier to use in food products globally because of regulatory approval and we've also added the Bitter Blocker BB68. So really being able to round out our portfolio from our perspective is very critical.

And then finally we're very excited about the progress that we're making from an R&D standpoint, both of these are more challenging projects that I'll reference the salt I think we made very good progress and really narrowing down the potential salty taste targets and I think that's represented by the new agreement that we have with PepsiCo and I think we're doing some really interesting things from a natural perspective too. So, I think all in all the company is very pleased with where we're at and the progress that we've made during the first four months of the year.

Hi, Scott. No, I wouldn't define it as seasonality. I would say using your words is more of a fundamental upside, again we're tracking to 10 million for this year of commercial revenues, so I think that puts us on pace towards that target.

Scott maybe just a little more clarity on that too if you look at the first quarter year over year, during 2013 we actually had a higher minimum annual royalty that we had from one partner before we reacquired rights, so really when you look at all other aspects of our commercial revenue we're growing in the first quarter compared to 2013 but please keep in mind that once guided that we think that the commercial revenue will be heavily weighted to the backend.

Yes, we booked revenues in the first quarter, Scott for direct sales.

Yes, the initial research funding period was four years and runs through August of 2014. PepsiCo has a one-time option to expand for two years and they'll have to provide us with written notification before the expiration at that time.

Yes, I think one of the benefits of being our partner with Senomyx is that you have earlier access to discovery efforts in other programs and certainly we do that with all of our partners where there is mutual interest. They have options during that to fund for up to an additional three years beyond that.

Thank you. The next question comes from [indiscernible] of Needham Company.

Unidentified Analyst

Just a couple of questions, I guess a few more on the new PepsiCo research collaboration. Just wanted to know, how it impacts your ongoing research in a salt taste program. And then I guess, the current terms for the collaboration for 2014, just trying to get an idea of timelines for extensions, I know they are similar to the other PepsiCo collaboration on the sweet taste program?

Tony Rogers

So on the efforts on the salt taste program, I think the first thing that I just want to continue to confirm is that, our number one objective is validating a salty taste receptor and we won't do that until we have a taste proof of concept.

We've worked through a proprietary database of over 25,000 proteins and the team's done a tremendous job with analytical approaches that has allowed us to get to a very manageable number of candidates for doing that. But we still have to do the work on those candidates and make sure one of those proteins or a group of proteins actually provides the desired impact on salty taste. So that's ongoing, we are doing work with that. We're screaming against those candidates and the challenge of research is you don't know until you found it, so the timing is very difficult to estimate. The way that the agreement is set up with PepsiCo is flexibility based on progress with the program where they would have the ability to extend beyond their initial funding period and that's really all the details we've disclosed at this point.

So if they've got an opportunity for sucrose reduction product which is a pot area then maybe that would take a lead in terms of working its way through the process. Thank you. The next question comes from Justin Ruiss of Sidoti.

Justin Ruiss – Sidoti & Company

Good morning. Thanks for taking my questions. Actually just had a quick one that kind of is a follow up to what was just answered before. But I know that with the direct selling program, it seems like you're kind of going through a lot of like paper work and regulation bureaucracy kind of, is there any way that you can accelerate that portion of it or does it just -- is that just the way that the flavor companies operate?

John Poyhonen

Well, maybe I'll start off and then I'll let Sharon hop in. I think that the flavor companies have -- all have a unique capabilities and proprietary technology and knowhow that they use with their customers and it takes time to incorporate our flavor ingredients into what they're doing. So we're very pleased with the amount of investment and resources that they're putting on the program. Ultimately though there is two ways they can gain the business, they can respond to a brief from their food and beverage clients or they can also do something more proactively where they try to push new solutions. And I think it will be a combination but obviously I think the quickest way is one they're responding to a brief since there is already an existing need that's been identified with a food and beverage company.

So we feel like they are going after this pretty aggressively, we've been pleased with the progress that they're making, but ultimately it all comes down to a food and beverage company deciding that they want to incorporate it into their products. And that's something that they don't have complete control over as well.

So I think that our turnaround here has been exceptional. When we're getting these documents our team is turning it over in a day or two so it's -- and these are 30 page documents. So we're pretty happy with that when they're asking for samples or placing orders, we're turning that around. The process is in place, so I don't know that there is anything that we can do to really accelerate that other than the fact that we are doing some efforts now also with consumer product companies from a pull standpoint where we're doing demonstrations and hopefully they'll try to pull that through their flavor companies.

Operator

At this time there are no further questions. So I'll turn the call back to Mr. Poyhonen to conclude.

John Poyhonen

I'd like to thank you all for participating in our call today and leave you with a few highlights regarding Senomyx's progress on our three key areas of focus for 2014. Our focus on Senomyx's patented key science technologies continues to drive our discovery activities and yield novel flavor ingredients that differentiate Senomyx from other flavor companies. We're making significant strides in our sweet taste program with new discoveries and promising advances with our natural flavor ingredients efforts.

We also welcome PepsiCo as our new partner on our Salt Taste Program. Senomyx's scientific expertise enables the continuing expansion of our pipeline of innovative flavor ingredients. Our Complimyx taste modulation ingredients brand now includes two complementary Sweetmyx sweet taste modifiers, a Savorymyx savory flavor and a Bitter Blocker. These offerings can address multiple challenges faced by the food and beverage industry.

Lastly, we've increased our efforts to the successful implementation of Senomyx's direct sales program. We are pleased with the positive reception of flavor companies to Senomyx's sales activities. We're building awareness of our brand while conducting evaluations at Senomyx's ingredients with key flavor companies, as well as demonstrations with selected consumer product companies that can help create demand for our offerings.

In summary, the first quarter of 2014 has been a promising beginning for the year and the company's future success. We thank you for your interest in Senomyx and look forward to updating you on our next earnings call.

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, this concludes our conference call for today. All parties may now disconnect.
