Meet The New NCT: CDO Recoveries, Golf And A 100%+ Upside Potential Over The Next 2 Years With A 10.6% Yield While You Wait

Jan. 13, 2015 8:51 PM ET  |  About: Newcastle Investment Corp. (NCT), Includes: SNR by: Dividend Investor 101

Summary

  • NCT expects to recover $350,000,000 to $400,000,000 in principal from its CDOs over the next one to two years.
  • This recovered cash, along with current cash and its golf portfolio, gives NCT a future book value of $7.38 to $8.13 per share within two years.
  • Management recently stated it believes the post-spin value of NCT is around $7 right now. With NCT currently trading at $4.50 per share, that's a 60% upside potential.
  • If NCT trades at a premium to book value, future upside is well north of 100% over the next one to two years.

Past History

A decade ago, Newcastle Investment Corporation (NYSE:NCT) was a high flying REIT investing in real estate primarily by means of Collateralized Debt Obligations (CDOs). During the Great Recession, NCT almost went bust. Since then, Fortress Investment Group (NYSE:FIG), has reinvented the company as an "incubator of new investing ideas".

Over the past two years, Newcastle Investment Corporation has been a spin-off machine. First with New Residential (NYSE:NRZ), then New Media Investment Group (NYSE:NEWM) and most recently with New Senior Living (NYSE:SNR). For those fortunate enough to have bought in about two years ago, there has been a 200%+ return when you add up the total value of the spin-offs and dividends from then until now.

If you did not have this knowledge of the stock and were just to look at its stock price and dividend history, you would think it was one of the worst performing stocks for the past few years, as its stock price and paid dividends have both gone down steadily since the spin-offs began in May of 2013. That is because of all of the spin-offs though, and not the poor performance of NCT.

A Bright Future

So what lies in store for Newcastle now? Fortunately, management has given us a sneak preview in their quarterly presentations and conference calls. Newcastle Investment Corp now consists of a legacy real estate debt portfolio that is generating a stable 15% yield and a golf portfolio with a targeted yield of 20%. They have $58 million of cash to invest, along with $82 million worth of equity invested in the golf portfolio.

(click to enlarge)

The legacy real estate debt consists mainly of CMBS, commercial loans, and bank loans. Total managed assets are $750,000,000 and are financed with debt. Management has stated that they expect to recover about $350,000,000 to $400,000,000 over the next one to two years from their legacy real estate debt portfolio because they took advantage of buying up some of these loans when prices where down a few years ago. There shouldn't be much of a problem recovering this money either, since they started 2014 with over $650,000,000 worth of recoverable principal and have already recovered over $300,000,000 in 2014 (most of the already recovered principal was put into the senior housing business that was recently spun off). The future recovery of principal will add a lot of value to future book value. Needless to say, NCT is currently trading significantly below it's future book value.

(click to enlarge)

While they have not come out and directly said what they plan to do with the recovered money, more than likely they will invest it in their golf portfolio, or the next best potential money maker that they see out there. Since on all of their investments and spin-offs in the past they have been able to generate a 15% to 20+% ROE, I trust that this time will be no different. In fact, they have already stated they expect to generate anywhere from 15% to 20%+ for the golf portfolio.

So, for the patient long-term investor who is willing to make an investment for the next few years, it looks like NCT will again be a home run if management is able to execute on its plans. The road will be a little bumpy on quarterly earnings though, since they will need to invest the money that they recover. Assuming this takes a few months each time, quarterly earnings will be up and down depending on how much investable cash is left on the table each quarter. (NOTE: I don't recommend putting a stop limit on the stock to sell it, as the price fluctuates month to month anywhere from 10%-15%.)

NCT's management also stated on the most recent conference call that they believe the golf portfolio is worth $300 million based on similar company valuations. They said once you take out the $150 million worth of debt, that would leave their equity value at $150 million. Not bad for an investment they just made about a year ago for $79 million. Assuming they are able to invest more money into the golf business and it does well, the shares should trade at a premium to book value. If future book value ends up being somewhere between $7.38 and $8.13 per share, as calculated above, that would mean future share holder value could be well north of the stated book value. My calculation above also doesn't take into account the higher multiple they believe they can get from their golf portfolio.

For investors with their eye on the future who pick up NCT for $4.50 right now, that would be an investment to be happy with a few years down the road... and while you are waiting, you will be able to collect the 10.6% dividend.

A word on the dividend

Newcastle recently announced a 4th quarter dividend of $0.12 per share. This equates to a 10.6% forward dividend. Before the 4th quarter and the most recent spin-off of SNR, NCT was paying a $0.60 per share dividend. After the spin, SNR has announced a $0.23 per share dividend and NCT has announced the $0.12 dividend. This amounts to a combined dividend of $0.35 per share. This is smaller than the $0.60 per share pre-split dividend. I believe this is because pre-split NCT was paying out about 100% of earnings on the dividend. It seems they have taken advantage of the recent split to readjust the dividend and have adjusted it lower. For SNR, this is probably because they want to save some earnings to buy more senior living properties. For NCT, management knows the quarterly earnings going forward will be up and down because of the recoveries in the CDOs, and also because golf is a season business. Therefore, the $0.12 dividend should be sustainable over the quarters while leaving some extra money for the company to grow the golf business.

Even if shares don't appreciate as much as predicted, and plans don't go as expected, maintaining the 10% dividend shouldn't be that hard based on all of the excepted cash they will be receiving.

Conclusion

Because of the sustainable dividend that is already pretty nice, I see limited downside for NCT in the future years and potential upside of 80% if it can hit $8 per share in the next two years. If it trades at a premium to book, there's a lot more than 80% upside, and this is not counting the dividends! If it were to trade at a slight premium to book value once all of the principal has been recovered, NCT's stock price should be at least $9 per share. That's a 100% return on your money if you are willing to ride out the next few years.

Potential problems

While I have laid out a scenario I think will happen above, there are potential problems that one needs to be aware of. The likelihood of these things happening is not very high though. For example, since most of the principal that they expect to recover is in real estate debt, if the real estate market were to crash within the next two years, recovery of some of the money that they had hoped for might not happen.

Management could also mess up and get a lousy return on the money that they recover. I doubt this would happen though, based on their track record of excellent returns. As I already mentioned above, it will also be a bumpy ride for quarterly earnings, as it will take some time to reinvest the principal recovered during the next two years. Money that is not invested immediately will of course be a drag on earnings.

A look at NCT's spin-offs

As stated above, I believe NCT will execute its plans to increase shareholder value and hit its 15% to 20% ROE targets. Here's a look at what they have done in the not so distant past:

Newcastle's most recent spin-off is New Senior Living, an operator of senior care facilities. They are all private, prepay facilities, so there is no risk from government payments being reduced in the future. Management believes it is fairly valued at between $20 and $22 per share, so at its current price of around $17 dollars per share, there might be some upside. Plus, management is planning on refinancing the debt at a more attractive price since it is a stand alone Senior Care REIT now, which will add some upside to earnings. They have plans to also aggressively grow the company by buying more senior care facilities. It's about 50% triple-net lease and 50% managed facilities.

In February of 2014, NCT spun off New Media, the largest operator of local newspapers in America. Since then it has doubled in price and also has a nearly 5% yield still.

One of my favorites is New Residential Investment Corp . For a more detailed analysis of NRZ, one of NCT's still undervalued spin-offs with a stable 12% yield and growing quarterly earnings, click here. Quarterly earnings are up 43% since it was spun off in May of 2013 and book value is up 24%. Yet the share price has not gone up. In my opinion, this is another great deal, especially if interest rates rise, since NRZ's portfolio will increase in value as interest rates rise.

For the patient, long-term investor, NCT offers a great dividend with plenty of potential upside. As always, remember to do your own research. Good luck investing!
