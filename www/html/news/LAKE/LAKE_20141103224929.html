Lakeland Soars As Ebola Hazmat Suit Orders Pour In

Thu, Oct 30 2014 00:00:00 E 00_WEB

By JAMES DETAR, INVESTOR'S BUSINESS DAILY

 Posted 10/30/2014 10:38 AM ET

  • Email
  • Print
  • Reprints
  • Comment

Industrial clothing maker Lakeland Industries' (NASDAQ:LAKE) stock price rocketed as much as 42% early Thursday after it said it will double production because it received orders for 1 million hazmat suits after the arrival of Ebola in the U.S.

Lakeland late Wednesday said it expects to continue ramping production to meet U.S. and global demand for protective gear to stop the spread of Ebola. The deadly disease has killed nearly 5,000 in West Africa and spread to other countries, including America.

Lakeland peer Alpha Pro Tech (AMEX:APT), which makes disposable protective clothing and infection control products, climbed more than 10% shortly after the market opened.

 View Enlarged Image

Lakeland's orders increase and manufacturing ramp-up have led to "a material improvement in our business," CEO Christopher Ryan said in a statement, adding, "At the same time, we have also been experiencing an improvement in operating conditions for the balance of our business globally."

The company said the financial boost from the orders will mainly come in its fourth quarter, which ends Jan. 31.

Production of its sealed seam ChemMAX and MicroMAX protective suit lines has risen nearly 50% since August on Ebola-related demand, the company said, and is on track for a 100% jump by January.

Lakeland's stock soared 47% to 29.40 on Oct. 13 on reports of the spread of the deadly disease and increasing orders for protective clothing, but had fallen back before Thursday's surge.

Lakeland shares were up 31% to 15.20 in Thursday morning trading in the stock market today.

Alpha Pro Tech, whose stock also spiked on earlier Ebola-related report, rose more than 7% Thursday morning.
