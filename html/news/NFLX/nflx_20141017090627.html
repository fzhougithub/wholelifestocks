3 Biggest Takeaways From Netflix's Latest Earnings Report

BY Chris Ciaccia |
10/16/14 - 08:19 AM EDT |
Tweet
Comment
Link
Stocks in this article: NFLXTWX
Find out if (NFLX) is in Cramer's Portfolio.

NEW YORK (TheStreet) Following Netflix's (NFLX) third-quarter earnings, the company's future is not in doubt as a service, but its earnings power may have been significantly over estimated, something investors aren't going to like.

In the third quarter, the maker of House of Cards and Orange Is the New Black earned 96 cents a share on $1.22 billion in revenue, below the $1.41 billion Wall Street was expecting. To make matters worse, the Netflix acknowledged that fourth quarter earnings would be severely effected, as the company has significantly more market expenses than it did in the third quarter.

Must Read: 10 Best Apple Products Ever

For the fourth quarter, Netflix expects to earn just 44 cents a share on $1.305 billion in revenue. Analysts are expecting the company to earn 85 cents a share on $1.49 billion in sales.

Netflix had previously said it expected to add 3.69 million subscribers, but the company was only able to add 3.02 million subscribers around the world, falling well short of its own forecasts just three months ago. Netflix ended the quarter with 53.06 million streaming subscribers, with 50.65 million of them paid. CEO Reed Hastings noted that the company over-estimated its growth this quarter, after under-estimating it in the prior three quarters.

"For the prior three quarters, we under-forecasted membership growth. This quarter we over-forecasted membership growth," Hastings said in the letter. "We'll continue to give you our internal forecast for the current quarter, and it will be high some of the time and low other times."

In addition, Netflix noted that the price hike in the U.S. is hitting growth a little more than it expected, as it only added 1 million streaming subscribers in the U.S., noting the "primary cause is the slightly higher prices we now have compared to a year ago."

Wall Street has been bullish on the stock, with one analyst recently putting a $600 price target on the stock, noting it could hit 100 million streaming subscribers by 2017. The company appears its way to hitting the 100 million mark (albeit slower than expected), and may do so at a much lower profitability than people on the Street are expecting.

In light of HBO's announcement that it will be available early next year without a cable subscription, Netflix noted that it does not think the two services will hurt each other. "Many people will subscribe to both Netflix and HBO (owned by Time Warner (TWX) ) since we have different shows, so we think it is likely we both prosper as consumers move to Internet TV," Hastings wrote in the letter. However  with the subscriber growth slowing, that line of thinking is now in doubt.
