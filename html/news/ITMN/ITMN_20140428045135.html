By Russ Britt

The 170% jump in InterMune Inc.’s shares Tuesday not only propelled the biotechnology company to unheard-of heights for a single session, but also lifted the boats of at least one index and an exchange-traded fund to stunning one-day gains and new records.

The First Trust NYSE Arca Biotechnology Index Fund /quotes/zigman/1497293/delayed /quotes/nls/fbt FBT climbed 5.72% to $87.45, an other-worldly rise compared with the incremental gains and losses exchange-traded funds usually deliver.

Shutterstock.com

The First Trust ETF is closely tied to the index in its name, the NYSE Arca Biotechnology Index /quotes/zigman/6017893/realtime BTK . That index made a corresponding gain of 5.75% to 2,946.43, thanks largely to InterMune’s massive price increase.

InterMune /quotes/zigman/85637/delayed /quotes/nls/itmn ITMN ended its phenomenal day with a whopping $23.84 per-share gain to $37.80. Shares rose quickly after the open as the company announced positive Phase 3 test results for a lung-disease treatment that had been rejected by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration, and it planned to refile an application with regulators in the third quarter.

Shares rose quickly after the open, dropped steeply in a hurry, and then began climbing steadily. They neared their intraday high when the closing bell rang.

InterMune, of course, is one of 20 components in the NYSE Index. First Trust’s ETF holds all components in its fund, but its tiniest slice is reserved for InterMune at just under 3.6%, according to the company’s website. The largest holding at just over 7% is in Myriad Genetics Inc. /quotes/zigman/58124/delayed /quotes/nls/mygn MYGN , which has made some sharp increases itself of late.
