LightInTheBox: Second Disappointing Quarter After IPO, Avoid This Stock For Now

  • Email
  • Print
  • Tweet
  • smaller
  • Larger

By Shuli Ren

Back in August, when LightInTheBox (LITB), a Chinese e-commerce that sells general merchandize overseas, reported its first earnings post IPO and disappointed big time, I asked what happened?

And it just did it again.

Third-quarter revenue came in at $68.1 million, below the consensus $69.9 million. Earnings per share was -$0.02, below the consensus $0.04, data from Factset show.

LightInTheBox expects fourth-quarter revenue to be $75 -77 million, below the consensus $83 million.

Apparel, which accounts for 27% of the revenue, was disappointing. Sales to North America slowed in the third quarter. Here is Pacific Crest analyst Chad Bartley on the details:

Apparel, which is 27% of revenue, continues to underperform due to increased competition, poor merchandising and poor execution.

Revenue from North America, which is 19% of revenue, fell 2% y/y, compared to +16% in Q2. Customers grew 75% y/y to 1.3 million compared to 140% in Q2. Revenue from existing customers, which is 35% of revenue, grew 92% y/y compared to 125% in Q2. Revenue from new customers grew 15% y/y compared to 33% in Q2.

LightInTheBox has a lot of structural headwinds, the largest of which is it sells mostly unbranded products and China is no longer a cheap manufacturing hub.

To be sure, LightInTheBox is not expensive by now. It is now trading at $7.7, below its ADR IPO price of $9.5, and is trading at 1x 2014 sales.

But with no catalyst in sight, it is not a stock we can bet on. Here is Pacific Crest again:

It will take several quarters for execution and business trends to improve, and for investors’ interest and confidence to be rebuilt.
