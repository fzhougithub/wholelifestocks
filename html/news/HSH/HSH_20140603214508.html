By Sue Chang and Ben Eisen, MarketWatch

SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) — Hillshire Brands Co. surged on Tuesday after a suitor stepped up its bid for the food company, while Krispy Kreme Doughnuts Inc. sank after the donut chain cut its earnings guidance.

Gainers


Bloomberg
Enlarge Image

Hillshire Brands /quotes/zigman/10406668/delayed/quotes/nls/hsh HSH +9.48%  jumped 9.7% as potential buyers courted the food-maker. Pilgrim’s Pride Corp. /quotes/zigman/13509380/delayed/quotes/nls/ppc PPC -2.24%  said Tuesday it would boost its bid to $55 a share in cash, raising the stakes after Tyson Foods Inc. /quotes/zigman/244186/delayed/quotes/nls/tsn TSN -3.04%  offered $50 a share last week. Pilgrim’s bid values the company at $7.7 billion, which is $1.3 billion more than its previous bid. Hillshire said it will hold talks with both bidders, but that it would not back away from its own plan to buy Pinnacle Foods Inc. /quotes/zigman/14664120/delayed/quotes/nls/pf PF +3.21% .

Applied Materials Inc. /quotes/zigman/19895/delayed/quotes/nls/amat AMAT +4.39%  shares rose 4.2%. Analysts at Jefferies initiated the stock at a buy with a price target of $28.

Dollar General Corp. /quotes/zigman/575788/delayed/quotes/nls/dg DG +3.89%  shares gained 3.9%. The discount retailer on Tuesday said its fiscal first-quarter profit rose to $222.4 million, or 72 cents a share, from $220.1 million, or 67 cents a share, a year earlier. On an adjusted basis, Dollar General would have earned 71 cents. Sales increased 6.8% to $4.52 billion.

Decliners

/quotes/zigman/258030/delayed/quotes/nls/kkd KKD 16.19, -2.81, -14.79%
Krispy Kreme year-to-date

Krispy Kreme Doughnuts /quotes/zigman/258030/delayed/quotes/nls/kkd KKD -14.79%  lowered its earnings guidance for the fiscal year when it reported results late Monday. The company said it now expects full-year earnings per share to be between 69 cents and 74 cents, below its March forecast of 73 cents to 79 cents. Severe weather in the Southeast led to a 1.5% decline in same-store sales for the quarter ending at the beginning of May, the firm said. Shares slumped 15%.

Wynn Resorts Ltd. /quotes/zigman/88601/delayed/quotes/nls/wynn WYNN -4.49%  shares dropped 4.3% on a slower rise in gambling revenue in Macau, according to Barron’s.

Apparel-maker Quiksilver Inc. /quotes/zigman/239314/delayed/quotes/nls/zqk ZQK -41.11%  said late Monday that its second-quarter loss was wider than Wall Street had expected, sending shares plummeting 42%. The company reported an adjusted loss of 15 cents a share on revenue of $408.2 million. Analysts polled by FactSet had expected a loss of 2 cents a share on revenue of $448.6 million.

Tickers to Watch

AAPL: Shares of Apple Inc. /quotes/zigman/68270/delayed/quotes/nls/aapl AAPL +1.41%  rose 1.3% after analyst Michael Walkley at Canaccord Genuity raised the stock’s target price to $710 from $660 on expectations of increased iOS product sales and potential for the company to grow its higher-margin businesses.

SPEX: Spherix Inc. /quotes/zigman/11916080/delayed/quotes/nls/spex SPEX +6.63%  shares rose 5.5% after the intellectual-property development company said it had completed a $20 million equity financing that involved selling 10 million shares of convertible preferred stock.
