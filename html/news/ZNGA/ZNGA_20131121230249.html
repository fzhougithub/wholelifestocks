Zynga Rides Momentum Wave

Nov 18 2013, 09:24 by: Bret Jensen  |  about: ZNGA
BOOKMARKED / READ LATER

Bookmarked

Readers of my columns know that I have had a speculative position in online & mobile game concern Zynga (ZNGA) for quite some time. I have been slowly building a position for this one-time social media darling since it crashed within six months after its much hyped social media IPO.

The stock has been one of my more speculative plays and it has required patience to hold through its tribulations, CEO transition and management departures in 2013. Lately, this patience has paid off and the stock has been impressively strong recently. The shares surged past the $4 a share level late last week for the first time since the summer of 2012.


(Click to enlarge)

The stock has seen positive momentum driven by a few themes/events in the last couple of weeks.

First, the company reported earnings and revenues for the quarter in late October that both beat subdued expectations. Zynga also reported it was basically cash flow breakeven in the quarter and it named a mobile industry veteran in the post of Chief Operations Officer, all of which reassured investors it was on an improving path to profitability.

Second, the huge success of online star Twitter's (TWTR) IPO earlier this month has buoyed the entire speculative online sector on a delayed basis. Chinese microblogging leader Sina's (SINA) Q3 beat and upwardly revised guidance has also been a positive for others in the online space.

The company is also starting to draw interest from several major investors. Steadfast Capital reported a ~5% "passive" stake in Zynga in late October and Jana Partners also has reported a significant stake recently.

Zynga's stock is up by a third since I last wrote about the company possibly following the success of another one time social media star, Groupon's (GRPN), turnaround efforts. However, I still think Zynga is a good very speculative play here and I plan to hold it despite the recent large rise in its stock price.

Zynga has several things that still recommend it as a speculative play in the online space besides its recent momentum driven rise.

First, it has a well-respected new CEO that came over from Xbox where he was very successful. This recent hire from Mr. Softie has 100 million reasons to drive a turnaround at Zynga and its stock price still higher.

Second, the company has a huge cash hoard of over $1.5B on its books. This is even more attractive now that the company is getting to cash flow breakeven status. This accounts for more than 40% of the stock's market capitalization. This gives the company tremendous flexibility in its turnaround efforts and also to make strategic acquisitions if prudent.

Finally, although the stock has doubled over the last year from its lows; it is down ~70% from its highs achieved in 2012 when it was a darling in the market. The online gaming space is an interesting space and could be very lucrative if the new CEO and his team can transform the company to be better positioned in this area. Zynga is still a very speculative investment for aggressive investors, but I still like the risk/reward here.
