    King Digital Insiders Have Cashed In For Almost $750 Million

    2 comments, 2 called-out
    Comment Now
    Follow Comments Following Comments Unfollow Comments
    Comment Now
    Follow Comments Following Comments Unfollow Comments

    When you read through King Digital’s IPO filing there is a line item in its cash flow statements and the last page of the document that describe dividends that were paid in October 2013 and February 2014 before its IPO. Combined with the IPO proceeds that company execs and insiders sold at the IPO they have received about $627 million.

    $504 million in dividends were paid out pre-IPO

    On the last page of King Digital’s 202 page IPO filing, page F-41, there are two sentences that describe two different dividend payments. On October 21, 2013, the Board of Directors declared an interim dividend of $1.050 per share that was paid on October 24 for a total of $286.7 million.

    The second dividend of $0.795 was declared by the Board of Directors on January 21, 2014, and a total of $217.1 million was paid on February 6.

    From the IPO filing execs and insiders held 96.4% of the shares pre-IPO. When you apply this percentage to the to pre-IPO dividends they received $485.7 million of the $504 million.

    Source: King Digital SEC filings

    An additional $120 million in dividends is going to execs and insiders

    When King Digital announced its June quarter results it stated that there would be $150 million in dividends (about $0.469 per share) paid to shareholders who own the shares on September 30, 2014. In addition execs and insiders would not sell any shares until after the December quarter results are announced in early 2015. Post IPO execs and insiders now own about 80% of the shares so they will receive $120 million making for a total of $605.7 million in dividends received by them.

    This compares to the company raising only $329.4 million in its IPO offering. The $276 million difference almost all comes from King’s 2013 earnings of $568 million since in 2012 it only earned $8 million and lost money in 2011.

    While mature companies pay a good amount in dividends I believe it is very unusual for such a young and what had been a growing company to be paying dividends. This feels more like a company owned by a private equity firm that is taking money off the table as it generates cash.

    $141 million of stock was sold by execs and insiders at the IPO

    It was well documented and disclosed that King Digital’s execs and insiders were selling stock at the IPO. Apax was the largest seller with 3.367 million shares, Melvyn Morris, King Digital’s Chairman, was the second largest seller with 851 thousand shares and CEO Riccardo Zacconi was the third largest selling 689 thousand shares.

    It is hard to find fault when the largest shareholders are selling less than 3% of their holdings to generate cash. That is one of the major reasons to start and grow a company so they deserve to enjoy some of the fruits of their work. However when you combine the dividend and IPO funds King’s execs and insiders have received it is almost $750 million.

    Short interest covering could help the shares in the short-term

    There was a large amount of short interest in the shares outstanding as of July 31 (12.28 million) vs. the approximately 25.5 million shares that were sold by the company and insiders at the IPO. Investors covering their short positions could help the shares in the short-term by not dropping as much as it would otherwise but I suspect the shares will remain under pressure until it becomes clearer if King can ramp any other games.
