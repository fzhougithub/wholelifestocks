    CFO Moves: Altair Nanotechnologies, MFC Industrial, Penn West Petroleum

    Altair Nanotechnologies Inc., a maker of energy storage systems based in Reno, Nev., named Karen Werner interim finance chief. Ms. Werner, who has served as Altair’s senior assistant controller, will fill the role vacated in January when Paula Conroy resigned. Ms. Werner’s current annual salary is $150,000, the company said in a regulatory filing; it didn’t provide further details. It also announced an interim chief executive to replace former CEO Richard Lee, who resigned last month.

    MFC Industrial Ltd., a Vancouver-based commodity supply chain company, appointed James Carter CFO. Mr. Carter has been a MFC vice president for more than 15 years. Compensation information wasn’t disclosed. Michael Smith, its chief executive and former chairman, had also previously served as CFO. MFC named director Peter Kellogg, a former dissident shareholder, as chairman, and said Mr. Smith would retire at the end of the year.

    Penn West Petroleum Ltd., a Calgary-based oil and gas producer, named David Dyck CFO, effective May 1. Mr. Dyck succeeds Todd Takeyasu, who is stepped down from the role. vice president of accounting and reporting Jeff Curran will perform the CFO duties until Mr. Dyck arrives. Penn West said Mr. Dyck was most recently CFO of a Calgary energy company it didn’t name, and before that was president and chief operating officer of Ivanhoe Energy Inc. His compensation arrangements weren’t disclosed.
