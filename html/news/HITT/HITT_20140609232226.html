Analog Devices To Acquire Hittite Microwave

Mon, Jun 09 2014 00:00:00 E 00_WEB

By DOUG TSURUOKA, INVESTOR'S BUSINESS DAILY

 Posted 11:57 AM ET

  • Email
  • Print
  • Reprints
  • Comment

Semiconductor maker Analog Devices (NASDAQ:ADI) announced on Monday that it has agreed to acquire Hittite Microwave (NASDAQ:HITT) for $78 per share, a 29% premium over Hittite's Friday closing price of 60.56.

The company said the deal gives Hittite a total enterprise value of about $2 billion, its market cap minus cash. With about 31 million Hittite shares outstanding, the deal is valued near $2.4 billion.

Hittite stock was up 29% in morning trading Monday, near 78. Analog Devices stock was up 5.5% in morning trading in the stock market today, at a more than 13-year high.

Hittite is a designer and manufacturer of integrated circuits, modules and other devices for radio frequency, microwave and millimeter wave applications.

Analog says the acquisition will be funded through cash on hand and short-term debt financing. Both boards have approved the purchase, which Analog Devices expects to close by the end of its fiscal Q3, which ends Aug. 3.

The transaction is expected to be accretive to Analog's earnings. Analog also reaffirmed its Q3 guidance. It sees revenue increasing by 1% to 5% year-over-year, compared with 5% growth in its previous quarter and 1% in fiscal Q1. The company expects earnings per share minus items of 60 cents to 64 cents. In the year-ago quarter, it posted EPS ex items of 57 cents on sales of $674.2 million.

"Hittite's strength in RF, microwave, and millimeter wave technology complements (Analog's) RF and signal conversion expertise," Analog CEO Vincent Roche said in a statement. "Our combined capabilities will enable us to bring more complete solutions to our customers and address more of the industrial, communications infrastructure, and automotive markets."

Hittite CEO Rick Hess said in the statement that he is "confident our combined efforts will accelerate the course of innovation throughout cellular and microwave communications infrastructure, automotive, industrial instrumentation, aerospace and defense."
